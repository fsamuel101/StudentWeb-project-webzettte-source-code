COLLEGE OF ENGINEERING

STUDENT ADMISSIONS

Admission and Retention

SECTION 1. Academic Performance - All students are expected to study diligently and maintain high academic performance. Each college must publish an honor roll at the end of each term. Any student who transfers to another degree program shall not be denied academic honors for which he is qualified under the provisions of this Code on Academic Honors. A disqualified student may be allowed to shift to another program at the start of any semester. A student may shift from one course to another as often as desired; provided he completes the course within the maximum residency period equivalent to the normal period fixed to the curriculum to complete the course, plus a grace period of one year

Available Degree Programs

Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering

Grade Requirements

Student applicants shall have obtained in Senior High a general weighted average of 85%% or its equivalent

Shall have obtained the following rating for each subject:
Mathematics – 85
Science – 85
English – 85

Physically and psychologically fit

Shall have a rating of 85% from the combined General Weighted Average (GWA) from Senior High and result of Entrance Exam with the following percentage: GWA 40% Entrance Exam 60%

Retention Policies

Maintaining General Weighted Average (GWA) for Major Courses for each semester must be 2.25 or higher

Must pass the Qualifying Exam at the end of 2nd Semester of 1st Year Students with the following subject:
Mathematics – 50%
Programming: Written Exam 25% Practical Exam 25%

• Failure to Qualifying Exam is ground for disqualification to the program and student will be advised to shift to another program

• First offense on the maintaining GWA will receive Academic Warning**

Two consecutive semesters of not reaching the GWA requirement is ground for disqualification on the program and student will be advised to shift to another program** ** Applicable only to 1st Year to 2nd Year Students

Third Year students who fail to reach the GWA requirement will have a choice of Academic Deloading (Only 75% of the courses for the semester will be allowed to enroll) or shift to another program
